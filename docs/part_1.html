Data Description

Identify your data source.

Observational collected data from local Monterey and CSUMB Esports tournaments, CSUMB library, and other non-random methods. Subjects voluntarily participated in a Purdue Pegboard test to measure dexterity, Psytool kit tests to assess simple reaction time, Go/No go test for impulsivity, and Poesner Cueing test for spatial orientation. Additionally, participants responded to a questionnaire describing hours of sleep per week, caffeine intake, and other items of interest.

Describe your data, including variables and data types.

Health and Lifestyle: Sex - Male or Female
Age_years Gaming_hr_week_ratio - Hours per week gaming Caffeine_Intake_mg_d - Reported caffeine intake (mg) per day Sleep_hrs - Typical hours of sleep per night Exercise_hours_week - Hours per week exercising Difference (Actual mean - Practice mean) of Cognitive measure test in milliseconds (ms): SR_ROC_Mean_RT - Simple Reaction test ROC_Mean_Go_RT - Go/No Go test ROC_Mean_Valid_Cue_RT_ms - Poesner Cueing test (valid cue) PP_ROC_Building_Test - Purdue Pegboard test Level of Gaming/Type: Gaming (measured as Non-Gamer, Casual-SB, Elite-SB, FPS, MOBA) Abbreviations: SB (Super Smash Bros Ultimate), FPS (First Person Shooter), MOBA (multiplayer online battle arena)

Identify the research questions you want to answer.

Does manual dexterity differ by amount of caffeine intake? Does manual dexterity differ by average hours a week exercising? Is manual dexterity influenced by simple reaction time?
